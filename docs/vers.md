# Poem: The Circle

In the night where numbers dance,
A point lands on the blank page.
It is the beginning, without emphasis,
A zero dreaming of infinity.

Then come the straight lines,
Segments that cut through space.
But the circle always returns,
Perfect, infinite, without end.

Its area is measured: pi x r^2
Its circumference: 2pi r
The uninterrupted dance of pi,
Spinning without ever repeating itself.

Inside the right triangle,
a^2 + b^2 = c^2
Pythagoras smiles in his tomb,
Knowing his theorem lasts forever.

And we, tiny points in the universe,
Search for relations, for links,
Like integers seeking one another,
To form pairs, sums, products.

Life, like mathematics,
Is a search for structures,
For patterns, for relationships,
And perhaps, one day, for meaning.
